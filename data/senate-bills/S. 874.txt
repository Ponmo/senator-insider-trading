SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.
  This Act may be cited as the `National Voter Registration Act of 1989'.
SEC. 2. NATIONAL PROCEDURES FOR VOTER REGISTRATION FOR ELECTIONS FOR FEDERAL
OFFICE.
  (a) IN GENERAL- Except as provided in subsection (b), notwithstanding any
  other provision of Federal law or any provision of State law, in addition
  to any other method of voter registration provided for under State law,
  each State shall establish procedures with respect to elections for Federal
  office to permit voter registration--
  (1) by application made simultaneously with an application for a motor
  vehicle driver's license pursuant to section 3;
  (2) by mail application pursuant to the provisions of section 4; and
  (3) by application in person--
  (A) at the appropriate registration site designated with respect to the
  residence of the applicant in accordance with State law; and
  (B) at a Federal, State, or private sector location designated under
  section 5.
  (b) NONAPPLICABILITY TO CERTAIN STATES- This Act does not apply to any
  State that has no voter registration requirement with respect to elections
  for Federal office.
SEC. 3. SIMULTANEOUS APPLICATION FOR VOTER REGISTRATION AND APPLICATION FOR
MOTOR VEHICLE DRIVER'S LICENSE.
  (a) IN GENERAL- Except as provided in subsection (b), each State motor
  vehicle driver's license application (including any renewal application)
  submitted to the appropriate State motor vehicle authority under State
  law shall also serve as an application for voter registration with respect
  to elections for Federal office. An application for voter registration so
  submitted shall be considered as updating any previous voter registration.
  (b) EXCEPTION- An individual referred to in subsection (a) may decline in
  writing to be registered by means of the motor vehicle driver's license
  application. No information relating to a declination under this subsection
  may be used for any purpose other than voter registration.
  (c) FORMS AND PROCEDURES- Each State shall include a voter registration
  application form for elections for Federal office as part of any application
  for a State motor vehicle driver's license. The voter registration
  application portion of such application--
  (1) may not require any information that duplicates information required
  in the driver's license portion of the form (other than a second signature
  or other information necessary under paragraph (3));
  (2) shall include a means to permit an applicant to decline, in writing,
  to register, as provided in subsection (b);
  (3) may require only sufficient information to prevent duplicate voter
  registration and to enable the appropriate State election official to assess
  the eligibility of the applicant and to administer voter registration and
  other parts of the election process;
  (4) shall include a statement that specifies each eligibility requirement
  (including citizenship), contains an attestation that the applicant meets
  each such requirement, and requires the signature of the applicant, under
  penalty of perjury; and
  (5) shall be made available (as submitted by the applicant, or in machine
  readable or other format) to the appropriate State election official as
  provided by State law.
  (d) CHANGE OF ADDRESS- Any change of address form submitted in accordance
  with State law for purposes of a State motor vehicle driver's license shall
  also serve as notification of change of address for voter registration
  with respect to elections for Federal office for the individual involved,
  if such individual indicates on such form the intent to change his or her
  address for voter registration purposes.
SEC. 4. MAIL REGISTRATION.
  (a) FORM- Each State shall accept and use the mail voter registration
  application form prescribed by the Federal Election Commission pursuant
  to section 7(3), for the registration of voters for elections for Federal
  office. Such form shall also be accepted and used for notification of a
  voter's change of address.
  (b) AVAILABILITY OF FORMS- The chief State election official of each
  State shall make the form available for government and private sector
  distribution, with particular emphasis on availability for organized voter
  registration programs.
SEC. 5. VOTER REGISTRATION LOCATIONS.
  (a) DESIGNATION- Each State shall designate appropriate State offices
  and (upon agreement with the Federal Government and nongovernmental
  entities) Federal and private sector offices as locations, with respect
  to registration for elections for Federal office. At each such location,
  the following services shall be made available: (1) distribution of mail
  voter registration application forms, (2) assistance to applicants in
  completing such applications, and (3) acceptance of completed applications
  for transmittal to the appropriate State election official. The individual
  assisting such applicant under clause (2) of this subsection shall not seek
  to influence the individual's political preference or party registration, or
  display any such political preference or party allegiance. Offices designated
  under this subsection shall include public libraries, public schools,
  offices providing public assistance, unemployment compensation, vocational
  rehabilitation, and related services, offices of city and county clerks
  (including marriage license bureaus), fishing and hunting license bureaus,
  and government revenue offices: Provided, however, That with respect to such
  offices that provide public assistance, unemployment compensation, vocational
  rehabilitation, and related services, all such offices in each State shall
  distribute, with their appropriate service application, either the mail
  voter registration application form provided pursuant to section 7(3),
  or their own form if their form is substantially equivalent to such mail
  form, unless the applicant, in writing, declines to register to vote. Any
  such applicant who does not decline to register to vote shall be provided,
  by such service office, the same type and manner of assistance with regard
  to the completion of the registration application form as is provided by
  such office with regard to the completion of its own application forms.
  (b) FEDERAL GOVERNMENT AND PRIVATE SECTOR COOPERATION- All departments,
  agencies, and other entities of the executive branch of the Federal
  Government shall, to the greatest extent practicable, cooperate with the
  States in carrying out subsection (a), and all nongovernmental entities
  are encouraged to do so.
  (c) TRANSMITTAL DEADLINE- (1) Except as provided in clause (2), each
  completed registration application accepted under subsection (a) shall be
  transmitted to the appropriate State election official not later than 10
  days after the date of such acceptance.
  (2) If the registration application is received within 5 days before the
  last day for such registration, such application shall be transmitted to
  the appropriate State election official not later than 5 days after the
  date of such acceptance.
SEC. 6. REQUIREMENTS WITH RESPECT TO ADMINISTRATION OF VOTER REGISTRATION.
  (a) IN GENERAL- In the administration of voter registration for elections
  for Federal office, each State shall--
  (1) assure that any eligible applicant is registered to vote in the
  election--
  (A) in the case of registration with a motor vehicle application under
  section 3, if the valid voter registration form of the applicant is
  submitted to the appropriate State motor vehicle authority not later than
  the lesser of 30 days, or the period provided by State law, before the
  date of the election;
  (B) in the case of registration by mail under section 4, if the valid
  voter registration form of the applicant is postmarked not later than the
  lesser of 30 days, or the period provided by State law, before the date
  of the election;
  (C) in the case of registration at a location designated under section 5,
  if the valid voter registration form of the applicant is accepted at the
  location not later than the lesser of 30 days, or the period provided by
  State law, before the date of the election; and
  (D) in any other case, if the valid voter registration form of the applicant
  is received by the appropriate State election official not later than the
  lesser of 30 days, or the period provided by State law, before the date
  of the election;
  (2) require the appropriate State election official to send notice to each
  applicant of the disposition of the application;
  (3) provide that the name of a voter may not be removed from the official
  list of eligible voters other than (A) at the request of the voter, (B)
  by reason of the death of the voter, or (C) as provided by State law, by
  reason of criminal conviction, mental incapacity, or change in residence
  of the voter pursuant to subsection (d);
  (4) inform applicants under sections 3, 4, and 5 of (A) voter eligibility
  requirements, and (B) penalties provided by law for submission of a false
  voter registration application; and
  (5)(A) to the greatest extent practicable, incorporate in application
  forms and other forms used at locations specified in section 5(a), some
  means by which the person completing the form may decline, in writing,
  to register to vote in elections for Federal office, and (B) provide a
  registration form to any individual who does not decline to register.
  (b) CONFIRMATION OF VOTER REGISTRATION- Any State program or activity to
  protect the integrity of the electoral process by assuring an accurate
  and current voter registration roll for elections for Federal office--
  (1) shall be uniform and nondiscriminatory, and
  (2) may not result in the removal of the name of any person from the
  official list of voters registered to vote in an election for Federal
  office for not voting.
  (c) NO SYSTEMATIC PROGRAM IMMEDIATELY BEFORE AN ELECTION- No general or
  comprehensive program, the purpose of which is to systematically remove
  ineligible voters from the rolls, shall take place less than 60 days prior
  to the date of a primary or general election for Federal office.
  (d) REMOVAL OF NAMES FROM VOTING ROLLS- No State may remove the name of a
  voter from the official list of eligible voters for elections for Federal
  office on the grounds that the registrant has moved unless the registrant--
  (1) confirms in writing that he or she has changed residence to outside
  of the jurisdiction of the registrar; or
  (2) has failed to respond to a notice from the registrar, as hereinafter
  provided, and has not appeared to vote and, if necessary, correct the
  address in an election during the period beginning with the date of such
  notice and ending on the day after the next Presidential election.
A State may, if it determines that a registrant may have changed his
residence, send by forwardable mail, a postage prepaid return card on which
such registrant may indicate his or her current address, together with a
notice that--
  (A) if such registrant did not change his or her residence, or changed
  such residence but remained in the registrar's jurisdiction, such card
  must be returned within 30 days, or confirmation of such registrant's
  correct address may be required before such registrant is permitted to
  vote in a Federal election during the period beginning with the date of
  the notice and ending on the day after the next Presidential election and
  that if such registrant does not vote in an election during such period
  such registrant's name will be removed from such list of eligible voters; or
  (B) if such registrant has changed residence outside of the jurisdiction,
  such registrant may no longer vote within such jurisdiction and, to be
  eligible to vote, must register to vote within their new jurisdiction.
The registrar is authorized to correct such lists in accordance with the
provisions of this section.
  (e) CHANGE OF VOTING ADDRESS- In the event of a change of address, for
  voting purposes, of a voter to another address within the jurisdiction
  of the same voting registrar, such registrar shall correct the voting
  registration list accordingly and such voter's name may not be removed
  from such registry of eligible voters by reason of such change of address
  except as provided in subsection (d).
SEC. 7. FEDERAL COORDINATION AND REGULATIONS.
  The Federal Election Commission--
  (1) shall be responsible for coordination of Federal functions under
  this Act;
  (2) in consultation with the chief election officers of the States, the
  heads of the departments, agencies, and other entities of the executive
  branch of the Federal Government, and appropriate individuals from the
  private sector, shall prescribe such regulations as may be necessary to
  carry out the provisions of this Act;
  (3) in consultation with the chief election officers of the States,
  shall develop a mail voter registration application form for elections
  for Federal office which--
  (A) may require only such identifying information (including the signature
  of the applicant) and other information (including data relating to previous
  registration by the applicant), as may be necessary to enable the appropriate
  State election official to assess the eligibility of the applicant and to
  administer voter registration and other parts of the election process;
  (B) shall include a statement that specifies each eligibility requirement
  (including citizenship), contains an attestation that the applicant meets
  each such requirement, and requires the signature of the applicant, under
  penalty of perjury; and
  (C) may not include any requirement for notarization or other formal
  authentication;
  (4) shall submit, not later than June 30 of each odd-numbered year, to the
  Congress a report assessing the impact of this Act on the administration
  of elections for Federal office during the preceding 2-year period and
  including recommendations for improvements in Federal and State procedures,
  forms, and other matters affected by this Act; and
  (5) shall provide information to the States with respect to State
  responsibilities under the provisions of this Act.
SEC. 8. DESIGNATION OF CHIEF STATE ELECTION OFFICIAL.
  Each State shall designate a State officer or employee as the chief State
  election official to be responsible for coordination of State functions
  under this Act.
SEC. 9. CIVIL ENFORCEMENT AND PRIVATE RIGHT OF ACTION.
  (a) ATTORNEY GENERAL- The Attorney General may bring a civil action in an
  appropriate district court for such declaratory or injunctive relief as
  may be necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act.
  (b) PRIVATE RIGHT OF ACTION- An individual aggrieved by a violation of
  this Act may provide written notice of the violation to the chief election
  official of the State involved. If the violation is not corrected within 90
  days after the notice, or within 20 days after the notice if such violation
  occurs within 120 days before the date of the election, the individual may
  bring a civil action in an appropriate district court for declaratory or
  injunctive relief with respect to the violation. If the alleged violation
  occurred within 30 days before the date of the election, the individual
  is not required to provide notice to the chief election official of the
  State involved before bringing a civil action under this subsection, but
  shall provide such official with a copy of the complaint within 24 hours
  after filing the complaint.
  (c) ATTORNEY FEES- In any action under this section, the court may, in
  its discretion, allow the prevailing party (other than the United States)
  reasonable attorney fees, including litigation expenses, and costs.
  (d) RELATION TO OTHER LAWS- The remedies under this section are in addition
  to any other remedy provided by law.
SEC. 10. ENFORCEMENT.
  Any person, including any election official, who in any election for
  Federal office knowingly and willfully--
  (1) intimidates, threatens, or coerces any person for (A) registering
  or voting or attempting to register or vote, or (B) urging or aiding
  any person to register or vote or attempt to register or vote, or (C)
  exercising any right under this Act, or attempts to do so; or
  (2) deprives or defrauds or endeavors to deprive or defraud the inhabitants
  of a State of a fair and impartially conducted election process through (A)
  the procurement or submission of voter registrations that contain false
  material information or omit material information, or (B) through the
  procurement, casting, or tabulation of ballots that are materially false,
  fictitious, or fraudulent under the laws of the State in which the election
  is held,
shall be fined in accordance with title 18, United States Code, or imprisoned
not more than 5 years, or both.
SEC. 11. FINDINGS AND PURPOSES.
  (a) FINDINGS- The Congress finds that--
  (1) the right of citizens of the United States to vote is a fundamental
  right;
  (2) it is the duty of the Federal, State, and local governments to promote
  the exercise of that right; and
  (3) discriminatory and unfair registration laws and procedures can have a
  direct and damaging effect on voter participation in elections for Federal
  office and disproportionately harm voter participation by various groups,
  including racial minorities.
  (b) PURPOSES- The purposes of this Act are--
  (1) to establish procedures which will increase registration of eligible
  citizens in elections for Federal office;
  (2) to make it possible for Federal, State, and local governments to
  implement this Act in a manner that enhances the participation of eligible
  citizens as voters in elections for Federal office;
  (3) to protect the integrity of the political process; and
  (4) to assure an accurate and current voter registration roll.
SEC. 12. DEFINITIONS.
  As used in this Act--
  (1) the term `motor vehicle driver's license' includes any personal
  identification document issued by a State motor vehicle authority; and
  (2) the terms `election', `Federal office', and `State' have the meanings
  given those terms in section 301 of the Federal Election Campaign Act
  of 1971.
SEC. 13. EFFECTIVE DATE.
  The requirements of this Act shall take effect on January 1, 1993.
  Amend the title so as to read: `An Act to establish national voter
  registration procedures for Federal elections, and for other purposes.'.
